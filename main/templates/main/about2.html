My Story


In 1996, my family emigrated to the United States. On the airplane, I turned 6 years old. My birthday gift was landing in a whole new world. Overcoming adversity with resolve, we prospered towards the American Dream; becoming citizens in 2006.

In 2012, my parents moved back to the Kurdistan Region of Iraq to retire. This was a time of peace in the Kurdistan Region, before ISIS. Three years later, after completing my degree in mathematics in the US, and amidst the invasion of ISIS, I decided to move back and support my younger siblings and my parents. Approximately 40 kilometers away from the frontline of the war, I was hired to be a Data Analyst for the American University of Kurdistan in Duhok, Iraq.

There, I began nurturing a passion for building databases from scratch and automating tasks around the office; working to streamline processes across several departments including the Registrar, Office of Finance, Academic Departments and Colleges, and the Human Resources Department. At AUK, I designed, built, and deployed an enrollment forecasting system and online course registration system (in-house) which enabled the university to operate more efficiently, automating hundreds of man-hours per semester and saving costs equivalent to 30% of tuition revenue in a single year. Currently working remotely, testing and deploying a University Management System complete with Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) software and a Student Information System (SIS).

In 2021, I moved to the Netherlands to be with my wife (Darstan). Here, I started a business intelligence and data analytics consulting company, Ferhadi Analytics. In doing so, I have invoked the Dutch-American Friendship Treaty and am now a resident of the Netherlands. I look forward to what the future will bring.
