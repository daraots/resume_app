About Me


In 1996, my family emigrated to the United States. In the airplane, I turned 6 years old. My birthday gift was landing in a whole new world. Overcoming adversity with resolve and hard work, we propsered towards the American Dream, becoming citizens in 2006.

In 2012 my parents moved back to the Kurdistan Region of Iraq. In 2014, after completing my degree in mathematics, and amidst the invasion of ISIS, I decided to move back to be with my younger siblings and my parents. Approximately 40 kilometers away from the frontline, I was hired to be a Data Analyst for the American University of Kurdistan in Duhok, Iraq. There, I began developing a passion for building databases from scratch and I started automating tasks around the office, often working to streamline processes across several departments including the Registrar, Office of Finance, Academic Departments and Colleges, and the Human Resources Department.

In 2021 I moved to the Netherlands to be with my Wife, here I started a business intelligence and data analytics consulting company, Ferhadi Analytics. In doing so, I have invoked the Dutch-American Friendship Treaty and am now a resident of the Netherlands.I look forward to what the future will bring.
